Our Traditional Food



Ethiopian food is very unique because we eat with our hands. We share a big plate as a sign of love and friendship. Sometimes we even feed each other, which is called a "Gursha."

The most important part of the meal is Injera. It is a sour, spongy flatbread made from a grain called Teff.

Dishes you must try:

  • Doro Wat: A spicy chicken stew. It takes a long time to cook and usually has hard-boiled eggs in it.
  • Shiro: This is made from powdered chickpeas. It is very popular during fasting seasons.
  • Kitfo: Minced raw beef mixed with spiced butter and mitmita.

The Coffee Ceremony (Buna)



Did you know coffee originated in Ethiopia? We don't just drink it quickly; we have a whole ceremony for it that can take hours. It represents hospitality.

How we make the coffee:

  1. Roasting: First, we wash the green beans and roast them on a pan over a fire. You can smell the smoke!
  2. Grinding: We crush the roasted beans by hand using a mortar and pestle (Mukecha).
  3. Brewing: The powder is put into a clay pot called a Jebena to boil.
  4. Serving: We pour the coffee into small cups called Cini. We usually eat popcorn or bread while drinking.

If you visit Ethiopia, you must try the coffee!
